Chokkalingapuram Meenakshi Chokkanathar Temple

Aruppukottai

INTRODUCTION


The Chokkalingapuram Temple, situated near Aruppukottai in Virudhunagar district, Tamil Nadu, is a divine abode dedicated to Lord Chokkanathar and Goddess Meenakshi. Bearing resemblance to the iconic Meenakshi Temple in Madurai, this sacred site exudes spiritual significance and architectural grandeur.


HISTORY


Legend has it that King Maravarma Sundarapandiya, prompted by his royal guru Paranjothi Sage, commissioned the construction of the Chokkalingapuram Temple to atone for his transgressions and seek divine blessings. The temple's inception is steeped in myth and devotion, with its origins dating back centuries.


THE TEMPLE


The Chokkalingapuram Temple stands as a testament to the architectural prowess of its builders, with a majestic Rajagopuram soaring into the sky and intricate sculptures adorning its walls. Within its sacred precincts, devotees find solace and spiritual rejuvenation, immersed in the divine vibrations that permeate the air.


Additional Information:

• The temple's Rajagopuram, adorned with five tiers and five urns, serves as a beacon of faith, welcoming devotees from far and wide.

• A visit to the Chokkalingapuram Temple is believed to confer blessings akin to those received at the famed Meenakshi Temple, with Goddess Meenakshi gracing devotees with her divine presence.

• The temple holds special significance during the wedding season, as couples seek the blessings of Lord Shiva and Goddess Meenakshi for marital bliss and prosperity.

• Devotees partake in rituals and offerings, paying homage to the presiding deities and seeking divine intervention in their lives.
