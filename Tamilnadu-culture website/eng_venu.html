Venugopalaswamy Temple

Karuvalur

INTRODUCTION


One of the unidentified brindavanas in Tamil Nadu is located in Karuvalur, approximately 38 kilometers from Coimbatore, on the banks of the Koushika River (which is now completely dried up). It is believed to belong to one of the 24 disciples of Sri Vyasarayaru.


HISTORY


The brindavana is situated within the vicinity of a demolished temple, believed to be the Sri Venugopala Swamy Temple, which dates back 1700 years. Unfortunately, the moola murthy idols are missing from this temple. However, visitors can still worship the uthsava murthy at a nearby local temple dedicated to Mariamman.


THE TEMPLE


The temple, despite its state of demolition, bears witness to its historical significance. Throughout the temple, one can find multiple carvings of Hanumantha on the pillars, adding to the architectural and spiritual charm of the place. Additionally, there is a separate Hanumantha temple nearby, cared for by the local community.
